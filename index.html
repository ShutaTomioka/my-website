Research & Projects

Aircraft Fuel Consumption Reduction through Climb Optimization

My graduation thesis at Kobe University focused on verifying the **fuel consumption reduction effect of optimized aircraft climb procedures using actual operational flight data**. I investigated the effectiveness of switching from the traditional VNAV SPD mode (Maximum Climb Thrust) to the V/S (Vertical Speed) mode during the mid-phase of climb, aiming to reduce engine thrust and improve fuel efficiency.

Using **four months of test flight data from Lufthansa Cargo's Boeing 777F aircraft**, my study analyzed the impact of external factors such as aircraft weight, outside air temperature (ISA deviation), wind speed, and wind direction on fuel efficiency. I developed Python scripts for **automated data extraction and cleaning**, ensuring high-quality data for analysis.

The research demonstrated a potential **fuel saving of approximately 35-40 pounds per flight** compared to conventional methods, aligning with prior simulation-based studies. This approach contributes to both economic savings and a significant reduction in CO2 emissions for airlines. The findings provide practical guidelines for airlines to select optimal climb methods based on specific conditions (e.g., lower temperatures and moderate aircraft weights for 600-4000 ft/min climb method).

AEON NEXT Senko Hanabi Coupon Project

As part of my professional experience, I contributed to the **AEON NEXT "Senko Hanabi Coupon" project**. This initiative aimed to significantly improve last-mile delivery efficiency for AEON's online supermarket "Green Beans". I was responsible for writing the **source code** for this project, which leveraged data science and AI to optimize delivery routes and coupon distribution, demonstrating a practical application of advanced analytics in retail logistics.
